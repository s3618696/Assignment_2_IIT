Ideal Jobs

Similarities

• Problem – Solving
• Communication
• Team building
• Analytical skill
• Planning
• Organization, critical thinking, research, management, negotiating skills, decision-making (technical project manager and business analyst)
• Creativity (IT Security Analyst and Software developer)

Differences

• Technical Project Manager must ensure the workflow and be responsible for the output of the team. Reporting, planning and quality assessment are the highly necessary skills for this job
• Business Analyst: besides analytical skills, business analyst must also have good research skills to identify risks as well as opportunities to leverage the business performance. Moreover, business knowledge about prices, revenues, and profits is also needed for this position, beside technology-related ideas
• IT Security Analyst: technology-related knowledge is required, as well as the ability to conduct test and detect for errors in the IT system. An IT Security Analyst does not need to know about business-related terminology as a business analyst does
• Software Developer: coding skills are the core requirement of this job. Moreover, there is no need for a software developer to write report or research for business innovation as a technical project manager or a business analyst
