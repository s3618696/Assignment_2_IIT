NLP and ChatBot (Quyen)

Definition, Current State, and Trend

Natural language processing (NLP) has lately received an awful lot of interest in representing and analyzing human language computationally. It has spread its packages in diverse fields such as gadget translation, email unsolicited mail detection, statistics extraction, summarization, medical, and question answering, and so forth. Dialogue systems or conversational structures consisting of chatbots, voice manipulate interfaces, and private assistants are examples of HCI (Human-computer interaction) software developed to engage with customers using natural language. Building a smart chatbot is difficult as it calls for contextual understanding, text entailment, and language-knowledge era. Consequently, various types of artiﬁcial intelligence (AI) and natural language processing (NLP) are required.

AI targets to make conversation between human beings and computer systems easier by using natural language. However, the complexity of human communication has resulted within the need for AI scientists to offer fashions that can understand human language and the usage of the NLP approach. NLP is the vicinity of research that explores the capability of computers to recognize the human language. According to Vic Duan (2018), founder, and CEO at Acobot, Innovating Ecommerce with AI, four main points can be represented in state of the art in chatbots and NLP technology, which will show below.

Natural Language Understanding (NLU): Chatbots can process user input in a natural language and react appropriately. AI chatbots and click bots are both popular in the industry. However, while click bots can only communicate with users through buttons or select lists, a chatbot with NLU technology doesn't require developers to list all viable expressions of a given consumer enter. For example, with the ability to understand the various ways in communication such as "How do you pricing", the bot will recognize it as "What are your prices" and respond appropriately. Therefore, NLU is very important to AI chatbot performance.

Contextual Conversation: This central feature of AI chatbot is also known as a multi-round conversation. When the chatbot provides a reply, it considers not only the current user input but also previous user or bot messages. Contextual communication is crucial because, during the interaction, humans appear to rely on context to communicate knowledge. For example,
• How's the weather in Ho Chi Minh now?
• It's raining.
• How about Ha Noi?
• Raining too.
In the second response, the bot considers its answer in the first question to output a different reply. Even though it is crucial to enhance the user experience by applying contextual conversation capability using many diverse methods, those methods are challenging to maintain and poor performance.

Memory: Many chatbots nowadays can memorize anything about users like name, gender, or age. Although it is fun and useful, they still have difficulty dealing with what they've spoken about with users. Vic Duan claims that the most challenging part is making use of the data in the chat logs storage.

Self-leaning: Anyone who has tried creating an AI chatbot would have felt the frustration of teaching a bot to answer questions. There are many questions users might ask, even though they visit a simple website or purchase a single product. Although AI chatbots can automatically communicate with end-users, building chatbots requires a great deal of manual work. The competent chatbots that can be used for business purposes are still too costly to be affordable.

Since most current work focuses on improving chatbot responses, we should explore the ability to interact using emotional and sentimental. Deep learning is a promising approach that could be applied to develop more effective chatbots. Additionally, the new AI chatbot generation should focus on using AI to create AI. For example, which can learn from the content, websites, and communicate with users in a completely autonomous way.

Because of the intelligence and convenience, NLP technology and AI chatbots bring in many benefits to many sectors of life such as economics, healthcare, and education. For example, the chatbots can automatically answer questions and solve problems that guests do not require "direct" personnel. Also, with being supported by natural language processing, NLP (the thinking language programming), the group's chatbot can detect visitors ' behaviors and preferences in the time to make "super-personalized" recommendations to meet all customer needs. Chatbots are consistent with many business models, from the clinic to the hospital, from the pharmacy to the health care spa. Health care organizations can be set up for chatbots to answer customers and patients with information about common diseases, give advice and automate multiple everyday tasks. NLP technology and AI chatbot are applying wisely in Vietnam, also known as ELSA Speak. ELSA was constructed using Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Natural Language Processing ( NLP) to cater to various native languages. This app offers instant feedback while you're speaking and provides a thorough analysis. It lists the strengths and areas which need improvement. Thus, it all proves that NLP and AI chatbot will affect everyone, especially for business and educational people.

Blockchain & Cryptocurrencies (Mai)

Definition, Current State, and Trend

Blockchain is a data structure using cryptographic techniques and is managed by a peer-to-peer community where everybody, each acting as a node in this chain, must together reach an agreement in validating and creating new transactions which will be stored in a new block. The data stored in these blocks also cannot be changed without the consensus of this whole distributed network, thus it will prevent hackers from modifying the information stored here (Chowdhury 2019).

Cryptocurrencies are a form of virtual asset which is only created limitedly, and the most well-known type of cryptocurrencies is Bitcoin, utilizing blockchain to store its value (similar to traditional assets such as gold, stocks or real estate). The prices of cryptocurrencies are determined by their supply and demand in the market. Cryptocurrencies can be used in transactions via the Internet, equally to traditional currencies, and it can be sent directly from the account of one to another, which means there is no intermediary required to authorize these transactions. (Grabowski 2019).

The most prevalent application of blockchain nowadays is in the financial system. The most well-known application of blockchain, as aforementioned, is cryptocurrencies. Cryptocurrencies, until now, have not been made to be legal in every jurisdiction around the world, since policy makers are still afraid of their risks of being used in illegal transactions for money laundering and terrorism (Library of Congress 2018).

Nevertheless, it is refutable that blockchain can help facilitate transactions of stocks, bonds, and derivatives in the financial markets. It provides digital payments and means of exchange cryptocurrencies such as Wirex, an e-wallet that supports the payment and exchange of both traditional and cryptocurrencies. Recently, Mastercard, a prominent payment service company has joined hands with Wirex to promote this new payment method via digital currencies, according to Browne (2020).

Blockchain is also used in the prediction marketplace system, where blockchain acts as an advisor to assist investors in predicting the future state of the market, and henceforth they can rely on these advice to trade shares, earn interest on market products such as oil, gas, or traditional currencies. The relevant examples here are Augur and Bitshares, both are digital tokens that apply PMS for users to trade based on the outcomes of future events (Casino et al. 2019).

Also, thanks to the transparency that blockchain brings to users, it has helped reduce costs to produce financial reports and financial auditing (Casino et al. 2019).

This technology is utilized in integrity verification as well, in which the system will store the data about lifetime information and transactions related to the formation of a product or a service: Blockchain has already been deployed for intellectual property protection (Ex: Mediachain, a database which helps creators claim and save their intellectual property rights) and for identity management (Ex: Shocard, a company which provides solutions to ease the complexity in “Know your customer”) (Casino et al. 2019).

Another field in which blockchain is nurturing is IoT, Internet of Things. Blockchain provides a way to secure data exchanged between interconnected devices, enhance their synchronization and their efficiency when operating in a decentralized and automated network (Ex: Blockpass, an application powered by blockchain technology to simplify the process of verification for humans, objects and connected devices when using different applications) (Jain 2018 & Chain of Things n.d.)

In businesses, many crowdfunding organizations have been seen to apply blockchain technology. The case of Swarm, Lighthouse and bitFyler can be mentioned here where they are all using blockchain to build their platforms (Casino et al. 2019). Another sector which is witnessing the rapid growth of blockchain is supply chain management, as the technology helps expand the value chains by developing trust between stakeholders, detecting counterfeit products, reducing paper works and the intervention of intermediaries.

Despite the fact that more and more people are aware of the usefulness of blockchain and apply it in their businesses, it is still far from perfect for blockchain to be widely used since it remains several issues which hinder the way entrepreneurs can deploy this technology on a large scale.

The first problem of blockchain is latency: each transaction of Bitcoin takes several minutes to process, due to the processing of each block and the security checks to eliminate double-spend attack, while it takes much more slower for traditional processing system of VISA to validate a transaction (Casino et al. 2019).

The second problem relates to the intensive power consumption that blockchain requires for the system to keep verifying transactions, since it costs about 15 million USD/day for mining Bitcoin (Krishnan et al. 2020). This leads to massive expenses to deploy and to maintain the capacity for the growing users, especially for small and medium enterprises (Golosova & Romanovs 2018).

The third problem of blockchain is scalability: when more nodes are added, the verification time will increase, and new blocks will have to be waiting for being validated. Notwithstanding, this issue can be tackled by Raiden Network Protocol, by which global consensus is not necessary because only the last on-chain transaction between each entity is charged a fee, other transactions can occur off-chain and require no fees. A network of payment channels between different entities can be established to utilize the inherent payment networks created so that the new channel does not have to be formed and thus reduces costs of using blockchain (Krishnan et al. 2020).

Giving such obstacles, blockchain is still believed to keep emerging in certain sectors. Insurance is a potential field for this technology to develop, as blockchain can help ease the process of validating claims in different types of insurance (Prabhakar et al. 2017).

Regarding the field of governance, blockchain-based applications can ease the process of managing public records as well as mitigating the risk of corruption and bureaucracy in the context of smart city through electronic voting and simplified procedure of taxation, claiming patent, and business registration.

With respect to the healthcare system, blockchain can be used to assure data security in managing healthcare records of patients (Casino et al. 2019).

With reference to education, blockchain can provide transparency in handling educational records to promote more equitable rewards (Casino et al. 2019).

Also, in businesses, there is still space for blockchain to evolve through ensuring illegal information disclosures through cross-organizational management and facilitating the selection process, increase the credential in applicant’s background, and ease the payroll procedure in human resources management (Wiles 2019).

Social Impact

It is manifestly true that blockchain was born to promote decentralization in many fields such as the Internet and many public sectors which are currently being conquered by Internet Service Providers, the governments and other tech giants. With the ubiquity of blockchain in the future, corruption and monopoly may be reduced (Al-Saqaf & Seidler 2017).

Besides, companies which are acting as intermediaries in transactions will most likely be negatively affected as blockchain technology and cryptocurrencies will foster the peer-to-peer exchange, allowing direct interaction between sellers and consumers without the need of their intervention (Ertz & Boily 2019).

The way an organization is managed will also be changed: major decisions can be carried out without the involvement of the CEO by a new governance system called decentralized autonomous organization (DAO), powered by Ethereum, where the votes of the members in the organization can be updated real-time through smart contracts and therefore enables every member to express their opinions about certain issues in the organization (Lee 2019).

In the world of blockchain, the money supply will no longer be adjusted by the central bank, as there will be more than one type of currency in a country and the interest rate of each currency will rather be determined by its degree of risk than deliberate plan of the central bank (Anderson 2019).

It should also be mentioned that thanks to blockchain and cryptocurrencies, workers, especially those who are working for multinational corporations, will get paid more quickly and receive relatively higher wage as they can avoid the imbalance in exchange rate between different countries as well as the cumbersome paper works issued by the intermediary banks (Hartog 2019).

The costs will be directly charged in my e-wallet since everything I own is linked to it. Also, if I am to lend the motorbike for my friend or my brother, they cannot use my e-wallet to fill up the tank because this system is customizable, and even in the case that I allow them to use my e-wallet, I can still track how much money they have used through the history transactions which cannot be made to change.

Also, my mother will not have to bother about prices and quality of goods when going to the market anymore: an app will calculate the best prices in every local groceries in the city, send the lists of goods she has chosen before to the retailers and they will send everything she needs to our home, and the bills will be automatically settled in my e-wallet (Huckle et al. 2016).


Cybersecurity (Loi)

Definition, Current State, and Trend

Cybersecurity refers to the act of protect data, information, networks, and other electronic devices from being damaged by “electronic attacks” (Fruhlinger 2019, para. 1).

Below is some common types of cybersecurity:
• Network security
• Information security
• Application security
• Disaster recovery
• Leadership commitment
• Operational security
• End-user education (Thakur & Pathan 2020, p. 32)

It should be noted that cybersecurity is not just a single action but it involves a long-term procedure of raising awareness, planning, governing, executing the plan and assessing its effectiveness (Thakur & Pathan 2020).

Current state

Cyberattacks

Common types of cyberattacks Description
Denial of Service Hackers try to congest the server with redundant messages to prevent the system from operating normally
Distributed Denial of Service Similar to Denial of Service, but rather than using one source of traffic, hackers will use multiple sources to attack the server at one time
Man-in-the-middle attack The connection between users is disrupted by hackers without their acknowledgement, thus the secret information communicated can be exposed and stolen
Crypto jacking Hackers will manipulate the processing power of the miner to hijack the cryptocurrency machines and receive rewards for crypto mining
SQL Injection Hackers will type in malicious SQL command on the interface of the application, taking advantage of the ill-protected of Active Server Page website, PHP applications, and SQL server forms
Spamming The practice of bombarding unrequested information to recipients for advertising purpose, for phishing, or even for the purpose of spreading computer malwares such as viruses, trojans, worms, or spyware
Cyberterrorism Electronic attacks to the government information system or the defence system of a country to obstruct the political framework or to pressurize politicians due to disagreement towards some public policies
Digital property misappropriation Unauthorized usage of intellectual property published online such as e-books, video, images… without the consent of the creators
Zero-day Exploitation Hackers attack the software and this digital invasion is unknown to the department in-charged, hence when the vulnerability occurs, there would be little or no time for the software engineers to improvise with the problems
Phishing Users are tricked to reveal their personal data to hackers through e-mails that are simulated to as those e-mails are officially sent from the banks or the users’ service providers
Digital vandalism Hackers will get control of the IT system, then manipulate, modify, or even destroy the data available in the system
Cyberstalking The act of assaulting an individual or organization on cyberspace, including false accusation, slander, defamation, blackmailing…
Cyber frauds and forgery The practice of falsifying and counterfeiting documents or files with the purpose of deceiving others

(Thakur & Pathan 2020)

Cybersecurity solutions
Cybersecurity practices Description
Intrusion detection system (IDS) A system involving devices, sensors and applications to recognize malicious activities on the network, the host operating system, or other registry files (Thakur & Pathan 2020). Usually an IDS will function as a data collector, then it will select the most salient features, analyze the data and report the detailed activities in the system (Sandvine 2016, Thakur & Pathan 2020)
Intrusion prevention system (IPS) Similar to IDS but is designed to automatically take actions based on the data analyzed to prevent any jeopardies for the network (Sandvine 2016, Thakur & Pathan 2020)
Device cybersecurity software Anti-virus software installed to the computers to scan for suspicious software and stop the spreading of such malware (Daniels 2020)
Firewalls Systems that help investigate the origin as well as the destination of the traffic in the network and acts as a defence if there are any threats coming from these traffics
Scrubbing centres A system that helps analyse, detect malicious traffics and remove them so as to avoid DDos attacks (Sandvine 2016, Radware n.d.)
Virtual private network (VPN) A channel that helps encrypt and secure communication via the Internet. Remote-access VPN utilizes protocols such as IP Security or Secure Sockets Layer to enable this authentication and encryption (Daniels 2020)
Data Loss Prevention (DLP) Tools, procedures and policies established to guarantee that confidential and sensitive data of individuals and organizations is not stolen, unauthorizedly modified and illegally leaked out (Zhang 2020)
Regularly update software/application This practice is necessary for devices that contain essential data or applications that can track the user location, because the updated software/application will have new features that patch the digital vulnerabilities of the previous version and can prevent the devices from being hacked (Tripwire 2018)
Algorithms and other techniques
  • Detection
  • Machine Learning: the model will be trained to recognize suspicious activities in the networks to anticipate and prevent the cyberattacks
  • Supervised
  • Unsupervised
  • Semi-supervised

  • Evolutionary: Genetic Algorithm => the artificial selection process will help in defining the metaheuristics, a series of practices which can “find, generate and select” a “heuristic” (a heuristic approach is an approach of finding new techniques to solve a problem better
  • Statistical: different algorithms will be combined to identify malicious activities in the IT system. These algorithms can be:
  • Entropy

  • Machine learning algorithms

While the algorithms used in the detection process are applied when the attacks have occurred, the algorithms used the prediction phase aim to diagnose possible vulnerabilities and flaws in the system where hackers can take advantage and disrupt the system

  • Prevention
  • Strictly comply with the security guidelines
  • Regularly update the software applications
  • Be aware of inside and outside threats
  • Always set up backup data
  • Emergency plan must be carried out to improvise with dangerous situations
  • Network traffic analysis: diagnose possible threats of attack arising from the movement of the traffic and implement predefined practices to prevent the malicious activities from taking place in advance

Trend

In the future, machines will be able to protect themselves from being attacked, thanks to the help of AI: algorithms will be implemented to train the machine to categorize activities in the network (Mezic 2020, cited in Mezic 2020).

Another trend to be used is User and Entity Behavior Analytics. The system can help detect unusual activities arising from internal environment such as the employees in the company or the entities including routers, modems, etc. This technique will be applied by the support of both human intervention and machine learning (Anderson 2020).

Social Impact

Cybersecurity has become more vital for businesses and countries nowadays as cyberattacks are detrimental to their data system, their workflow, leading to confidential information leakage or bring about financial losses (Thakur & Pathan 2020). Moreover, since the process of digitalization occurs more and more rapidly nowadays, which means people cannot avoid technology adoption in their businesses, and this advancement in technology also comes with new threats in the cyberspace as well, which requires firms to be more updated and more proactive in securing their IT system. According to Thakur & Pathan (2020), only one cybersecurity breach in 2018 has already cost businesses approximately US$4 million. However, deploying a high-quality security system is usually unaffordable to a small-and-medium enterprise. Thus, such SMEs must build their own cybersecurity policies and strictly follow standard guidelines stated by regulatory authorities so as to deal with the increasing risk of cyberattacks in the future (Thakur & Pathan 2020).

Autonomous vehicles (Long)

Definition

Self-driving vehicles, known as autonomous vehicles, consist of technologies that allow a car to drive itself without human intervention use. It is developing for changing the world transportations. This innovative type of vehicle can help people to solve energy consumption, and pollution through reducing congestion.

Current state

Autonomous vehicles use sensors and cameras to alert the user when the user wants to change the lane or fast up so as to avoid accidents. Additionally, with the help of AI, these sensors can be connected together to simultaneously operate the car. Many sensors and cameras communicate and interact with each other to control the vehicle in the right way. For example, the Tesla model X with sensors to control the auto light, and alert for the user when have another car around. Then the camera will be used keep the car stay on the lane of the road and it can detect people in front of and behind the car. AI is also implemented to utilize the map and GPS and navigate the car more effectively.

Safety is the goal for every technology that has been made for people. There are a lot of inventions which help people more safety and faster such as 360 cameras for cars, sensors. Autonomous vehicles were born to connect everything above together which make the perfect vehicle that everyone can travel on.

Trend

Autonomous vehicles ability are separated into 5 levels, with level 0 is totally human drive and level 5 is the vehicle can drive itself without any help of human. Currently, the autonomous vehicle for consumption just stays at level 3, namely the Model X of Tesla, empowered by the Autopilot features to be semi-driven. With this system deployed in the cars, they can automatically steer, modify the speed, detect obstacles, tap the brakes when needed or find a place for parking with the assistance of forward-looking radar, eight cameras to ensure the optimize the view coverage, ultrasonic sonar to detect obstacles and GPS (Heath 2018). However, level-3-autonomous car only performs well on a good-conditioned road and is only automatic under the speed of 60km or below (Thomson & Granath 2020). Level 4 is being carried out by Waymo, which includes three-dimensional map with the information of road profiles, lane markers, crossroads,… and sensors which help detect not only objects around but also traffic light colours, traffic signs and railroad crossing gates… (Waymo 2020). However, just as level 3 car, the autonomous vehicle of level 4 only functions smoothly on an ideal road (Thomson & Granath 2020). In the future, level 5 vehicle will be developed with fully automatic features which the car can totally drive itself irrespective of the road conditions or other climate issues with the assistance of AI that will be robust enough to process a huge amount of data quickly to immediately improvise with unexpected events on the road (Unrau & Granath 2020).

Social Impact

The world is changing every day and people have become busier to adapt with this lifestyle. It means that they have no time to do basic things like eating breakfast or dressing in the complete way. Therefore, the demand for full automation cars is increasing because they can let the car self-drive and tie their tie or eat breakfast on the way to their company at the same time. Moreover, it is the transportation companies that will gain more profits thanks to this technology, as there will be no need to hire drivers anymore. Since machines make fewer errors and are capable of working for a longer of time than humans, the companies which adopt these autonomous vehicles in their businesses will be able to operate more efficiently. On the other hand, it will force those who work as drivers to switch to another job. This is also a challenge to be faced by policymakers, as the number of people working in this sector is quite large and usually, they are in low-income class. The trade-off between unemployment and productivity should be tackled ingeniously so that the wealth gap in society will not get wider.

Nevertheless, everything in the world always has its own pros and cons. Unemployment may be inevitable, but for a more innovative and productive world where our living standard should be improved every day by new technologies, a short-run shift in the labour force as discussed above can bring about a more desirable outcome for everyone in the society.

Personal Impact

In addition, I hope this technology will be perfect as soon as it can because my mom wants to have a private driver, but hiring a private driver is not always an ideal choice for our family’s private trip. Besides, since it is not distracted by other events along the street and its decisions are more precise than humans, as those decisions are made based on real-time data collected from the surrounding environment, there can be no doubt than autonomous vehicles will be safer than traditional personal driving.
